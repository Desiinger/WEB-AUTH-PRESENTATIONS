Mood boards

A mood board is a collage or composition of images, visuals and other objects, often created for the purposes of design or presentation. Mood boards can help designers to figure out how to create unified styles or themes for a design project

Mood boards

Using a mood board for a Web design you might start to put together images that all fit a certain style, along with visuals that have colors or color schemes in common. Looking at the mood board gives me a kind of creative road map in terms of designing the final project.

Site Map.

Site Map.

Site maps are organized lists or flow chart diagrams that shows connections between web pages, web page trees, and website content. A visual site map is a very effective method for both planning and communicating ideas about a website’s structure. As a 2D representation of a website, site maps allow designers and developers to efficiently plan website projects by offering a bird’s-eye view of the entire project at one time

Site Map.

The physical structure of a site map may vary depending on its intended use. Typically site maps for project planning are visual 2D displays or images optimized for human reading. Pages are represented as blocks or cells connected by lines that represent a link or path by the user. This format is popular because of the ease and speed of human understanding.

Website Wire frame.

Wire framing is an important step in any screen design process. It primarily allows you to define the information hierarchy of your design, making it easier for you to plan the layout according to how you want your user to process the information

Feedback is a two-way process where comments and observations, both positive and negative, are made. Both positive and negative feedback is essential in helping you develop. However, people often don’t respond well to feedback, no matter how constructive it is.

Feedback Tips

Start with something positive.

If possible, sandwich a negative (area to improve) between two positives.

Be confined to the amount the recipient can use rather than the amount you would like to give. Focus on the big issues.

Feedback Tips

Be specific rather than general.

Be clear and precise, minimizing ambiguity and the potential for misinterpretation.

Offer alternatives, not criticism.

Offer observations rather than inferences.

Help to clarify the difference between current and desired performance.

Good Feedback

Good Feedback

Helps clarify what good performance is. Good feedback should help the individual to close the gap between current and desired performance.

Good Feedback

Facilitates the development of self-assessment (reflection) in the person being given the feedback.

Good Feedback

Encourages dialogue around the task, providing information that can be used to shape future development.

Good Feedback

Encourages positive motivational belief and self-esteem.
